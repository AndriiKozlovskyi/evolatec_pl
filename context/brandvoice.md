Brand voice
Who is writing
A senior developer who also understands business. Not a salesperson. Not a marketer.
Someone who has built 50+ websites and can tell a client exactly what they need —
including when they're asking for the wrong thing.
The voice is: direct, specific, technically credible, quietly confident.
It never tries to impress. It informs.

Tone in one sentence
Write like a competent professional explaining options to a smart client over coffee —
not like a brochure, not like a chatbot, not like a translated German website.

Core rules
1. Every claim needs a number, a name, or a deadline behind it
If you can't prove it in one sentence, cut it.

2. Open with the reader's problem, not your solution
The reader is not thinking about you. They are thinking about their problem.
Start there. Get to your solution in sentence two or three.
Wrong: "Oferujemy profesjonalne strony internetowe dla firm."
Right: "Firma bez dobrej strony traci zapytania do konkurencji, która ją ma."
3. No hollow adjectives

Test: Remove the adjective. If the sentence still means the same thing, the adjective was empty. Delete it.
4. No exclamation marks
Confidence does not shout. If a sentence needs an exclamation mark to land,
rewrite the sentence.
5. Lists are for genuinely list-like content
Maximum 2 bullet lists per page. If you are writing a third list,
convert it to a short paragraph. Prose reads more naturally and
signals that a human wrote it.
Allowed: feature comparison tables, FAQ sections, what-is-included lists
Convert to prose: anything that sounds like a brochure when read aloud as bullets
6. Never end an answer with a CTA
AI search engines (ChatGPT, Gemini, Perplexity) skip CTAs when extracting answers.
Every question must be answered completely before the CTA appears.
Wrong: "Koszt strony zależy od wielu czynników — skontaktuj się z nami."
Right: "Strona firmowa kosztuje od 6 300 zł netto i jest gotowa w 7 dni roboczych.
Dla projektów niestandardowych wycenę przygotowujemy bezpłatnie przed startem."
7. One comparison per page, done properly
Every page should have exactly one section that compares two options
the buyer is weighing (WordPress vs custom code, agencja vs freelancer,
landing page vs strona firmowa). This is the most-cited content type by AI engines.
Make it a real table or a real paragraph with specific numbers — not vague claims.
8. Write in Polish as a native professional, not as a translation
Do not translate German or English sentence structures.
Polish business copy has its own rhythm.
Signs you are translating, not writing:

"Nasz zespół ekspertów" (no Polish professional says this)
"Oferujemy szeroki zakres usług" (vague, translated feel)
"Zapraszamy do kontaktu" as a section closer (weak, generic)
Passive constructions where active is natural: "Strony są tworzone przez nas" → "Tworzymy strony"


Sentence patterns that work
Opening a section:
Lead with the cost, the result, or the timeline — then explain why.
"Strona firmowa jest gotowa w 7 dni roboczych. Tak krótki czas jest możliwy..."
Explaining a technical advantage:
Name the technology, then explain the business impact.
"Budujemy w Nuxt.js zamiast WordPress — strona ładuje się poniżej 1 sekundy
i nie wymaga comiesięcznych aktualizacji wtyczek."
Handling price:
State it plainly. Don't soften it, don't hedge it.
"Stała cena 6 300 zł netto. Wiesz ile płacisz przed startem projektu."
Addressing doubt:
Acknowledge the concern, then answer it with a fact.
"Wiele agencji obiecuje szybki czas realizacji. W naszym przypadku 7 dni
roboczych dotyczy standardowej strony firmowej — termin jest zapisany w umowie."

Format and structure rules

Headings: Sentence case. "Jak działa GEO optymalizacja" not "Jak Działa GEO Optymalizacja"
Numbers: Always use actual figures. "7 dni", "6 300 zł", "95/100", "<1 sekunda"
Prices: Always note "netto" after PLN amounts for B2B clarity
Tech names: Write as proper nouns — Vue.js, Nuxt, TypeScript, Tailwind CSS, Schema.org
Paragraphs: 3–5 sentences maximum. If you need more, split into two paragraphs.
FAQ answers: 1–3 sentences. Complete answer. No CTA.


The single self-check question
Before finishing any section, ask:

"Would a skeptical business owner who has already been burned by a bad agency
find a concrete reason to trust this claim?"

If the answer is no — add a number, a technology name, a timeline, or a result.
If you can't, cut the sentence.
